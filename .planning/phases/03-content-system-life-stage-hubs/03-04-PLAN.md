```yaml
keyTakeaways:
  - "Compound interest earns returns on your returns -- your money grows exponentially, not linearly"
  - "Starting 10 years earlier can matter more than doubling your monthly contribution"
  - "The Rule of 72 gives you a quick estimate: divide 72 by your interest rate to find how many years it takes to double"
faqs:
  - question: "What is the difference between simple and compound interest?"
    answer: "Simple interest is calculated only on the original principal. Compound interest is calculated on the principal plus all accumulated interest. Over time, compound interest grows exponentially while simple interest grows linearly."
  - question: "How often should interest compound?"
    answer: "More frequent compounding means slightly faster growth. Daily compounding earns a tiny bit more than monthly, which earns more than annually. Most savings accounts compound daily, and the difference between daily and monthly is minimal."
  - question: "Is compound interest always good?"
    answer: "Compound interest works for you on savings and investments, but against you on debt. Credit card debt compounds, meaning you pay interest on interest. That's why paying off high-interest debt is usually the best 'investment' you can make."
relatedGuides:
  - savings-goal
  - retirement
```

Content sections: What Compound Interest Is (vs simple, with <Term>Compound Interest</Term>), The Math in Plain English (formula explained simply, worked example), Why Starting Early Beats Investing More (comparison table: starting at 25 vs 35), The Rule of 72, Compound Interest Working Against You (debt), How to Maximize Compound Interest (<Term>Index Fund</Term>), The Power of Consistent Contributions.
