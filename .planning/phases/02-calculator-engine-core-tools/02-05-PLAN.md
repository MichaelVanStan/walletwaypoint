**1. retirement.ts** (TOOL-06):

Export `calculateRetirement(params: Record<string, number>): CalculatorResults`

Input params: `age`, `retireAge`, `savings`, `monthly`, `returnRate`, `withdrawalRate`

Computation:
- Accumulation phase (age to retireAge):
  - yearsToRetirement = retireAge - age
  - Use FV formula: retirementBalance = fv(savings, monthly, returnRate, yearsToRetirement)
  - Generate year-by-year accumulation data
- Distribution phase (retireAge to retireAge + 30, assume 30 years of retirement):
  - annualWithdrawal = retirementBalance * (withdrawalRate / 100)
  - monthlyWithdrawal = annualWithdrawal / 12
  - Model balance depletion year by year: each year subtract withdrawal and apply reduced return rate (returnRate - 1% for conservative post-retirement)
  - yearsUntilDepleted = years until balance <= 0 (or 30+ if sustainable)
- totalContributed = savings + monthly * yearsToRetirement * 12
- interestEarned = retirementBalance - totalContributed

Chart data -- retirementProjection (dataKey: "retirementProjection"):
- Area chart: `[{ age: 30, balance: 50000 }, { age: 31, balance: ... }, ..., { age: 65, balance: peak }, { age: 66, balance: declining }, ...]`
- Two visual phases: accumulation (growing) and distribution (declining)

Outputs: retirementBalance, annualWithdrawal, monthlyWithdrawal, yearsUntilDepleted, totalContributed

Interpretation: `By saving ${formatCurrency(monthly)}/mo from age ${age} to ${retireAge} at ${returnRate}% return, you would have ${formatCurrency(retirementBalance)}. At a ${withdrawalRate}% withdrawal rate, that provides ${formatCurrency(monthlyWithdrawal)}/mo for an estimated ${yearsUntilDepleted} years.`

**1. rent-vs-buy.ts** (TOOL-09):

Export `calculateRentVsBuy(params: Record<string, number>): CalculatorResults`

Input params: `rent`, `price`, `dp` (down payment %), `rate`, `years`, `appreciation` (annual home appreciation %), `rentIncrease` (annual rent increase %)

Computation using decimal.js-light:
- Buy costs year by year:
  - downPayment = price * (dp / 100)
  - loanAmount = price - downPayment
  - monthlyMortgage = pmt(loanAmount, rate, 30) (assume 30yr mortgage)
  - closingCosts = price * 0.03 (typical 3%)
  - Annual: mortgage payments + property tax (1.1% of price) + insurance ($1500/yr) + maintenance (1% of price/yr)
  - Cumulative buy cost = sum of all annual costs over `years`
  - Home equity gained: homeValue at year N = price * (1 + appreciation/100)^N, equity = homeValue - remainingBalance - downPayment - closingCosts
  - Net buy cost = cumulativeBuyCosts - equityGained + sellingCosts (6% of final home value)

- Rent costs year by year:
  - Year 1: rent * 12
  - Year 2: rent * (1 + rentIncrease/100) * 12
  - And so on
  - Opportunity cost: if downPayment + closingCosts were invested at 7% return, what would they be worth?
  - Net rent cost = cumulativeRentCosts - investmentGains

- recommendation: "buy" if netBuyCost < netRentCost, "rent" otherwise
- savingsAmount = abs(netBuyCost - netRentCost)

Chart data -- cumulativeCost (dataKey: "cumulativeCost"):
- Area chart: `[{ year: 1, renting: X, buying: Y }, { year: 2, ... }, ...]`

Outputs: totalRentCost, totalBuyCost, netRentCost, netBuyCost, savingsAmount, recommendation (1 for buy, 0 for rent -- display as text in interpretation)

Interpretation: `Over ${years} years, renting costs ${formatCurrency(netRentCost)} net while buying costs ${formatCurrency(netBuyCost)} net. ${recommendation === 1 ? 'Buying' : 'Renting'} saves you ${formatCurrency(savingsAmount)}.`

**2. student-loan.ts** (TOOL-10):

Export `calculateStudentLoan(params: Record<string, number>): CalculatorResults`

Input params: `balance`, `rate`, `income` (annual gross income for IDR calculation)

Compute three repayment plans:

**Standard (10-year fixed):**
- monthlyPayment = pmt(balance, rate, 10)
- totalCost = monthlyPayment * 120
- totalInterest = totalCost - balance
- Months to payoff = 120

**Graduated (starts low, increases every 2 years):**
- Initial payment = ~60% of standard payment
- Payment increases 10% every 2 years over 10 years
- Generate month-by-month: for months 1-24 pay initial, months 25-48 pay initial*1.1, months 49-72 pay initial*1.21, etc.
- Each month: interest = balance * rate/100/12, principal = payment - interest, if principal < 0 add negative to balance
- totalCost = sum of all payments
- Note: graduated may take slightly longer if early payments don't cover interest

**Income-Driven (IDR - simplified SAVE/REPAYE model):**
- discretionaryIncome = max(income - 15700 * 1.5, 0) (150% of poverty line for single person ~$23,550, simplified)
- monthlyPayment = max(discretionaryIncome * 0.10 / 12, 0) (10% of discretionary income)
- If monthlyPayment < interest accruing: balance grows (negative amortization)
- Cap at 20 years (240 months) -- remaining balance forgiven
- totalPaid = sum of payments over repayment period
- forgiven = remaining balance at month 240 (if any)
- totalCost = totalPaid (forgiven amount is taxable but not a "cost" to borrower in simple model)

Chart data -- planComparison (dataKey: "planComparison"):
- Bar chart: `[{ plan: 'Standard', totalCost: X, monthlyPayment: standardMonthly }, { plan: 'Graduated', totalCost: Y, monthlyPayment: graduatedInitial }, { plan: 'Income-Driven', totalCost: Z, monthlyPayment: idrMonthly }]`

Chart data -- paymentOverTime (dataKey: "paymentOverTime"):
- Line chart: `[{ month: 1, standard: X, graduated: Y, idr: Z }, ...]` showing monthly payment amounts over time

Outputs: standardMonthly, standardTotal, graduatedInitial, graduatedTotal, idrMonthly, idrTotal, idrForgiven

Detail rows: plan comparison table

Interpretation: `For a ${formatCurrency(balance)} student loan at ${rate}%: Standard repayment costs ${formatCurrency(standardMonthly)}/mo (${formatCurrency(standardTotal)} total). Income-driven at ${formatCurrency(income)}/yr income costs ${formatCurrency(idrMonthly)}/mo with ${formatCurrency(idrForgiven)} potentially forgiven after 20 years.`
