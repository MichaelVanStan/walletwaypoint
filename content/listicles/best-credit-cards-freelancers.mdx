If you freelance, your credit card should work as hard as you do. The right card can simplify tax season, earn you cash back on the office supplies and software you already buy, and keep your business expenses separate from personal spending.

We evaluated dozens of business and personal credit cards and picked five that stand out for self-employed workers. Each one was chosen based on reward rates on common freelancer expenses, annual fee value, and features that help independent professionals manage their money.

---

## 1. Chase Ink Business Cash -- Best Overall for Freelancers

The Chase Ink Business Cash earns 5% cash back at office supply stores and on internet, cable, and phone services (on up to $25,000 per year), plus 2% at gas stations and restaurants. That 5% category is tailor-made for freelancers who pay for coworking spaces, cloud storage, and SaaS tools.

**Why it stands out for freelancers:** The $0 annual fee means it costs nothing to keep, and the 5% on internet and phone services is money back on expenses most freelancers pay every month.

**Pros:**
- $0 annual fee
- 5% back on internet, cable, phone, and office supply stores
- 2% at gas stations and restaurants
- Free employee cards with spending limits

**Cons:**
- 5% and 2% categories capped at $25,000/year combined
- No travel transfer partners (cash back only)
- Requires a business (sole proprietorship counts)

**Key specs:** 5%/2%/1% rewards tiers | $0 annual fee | $750 bonus after $6,000 in 3 months

[Get the Chase Ink Business Cash](/go/chase-ink-business-cash)

## 2. Capital One Spark Cash Plus -- Best for High-Spending Freelancers

If your freelance business has significant monthly expenses, the Capital One Spark Cash Plus earns an unlimited 2% cash back on every purchase with no category restrictions. There is no spending cap, so high-revenue freelancers get the full 2% whether they spend $5,000 or $50,000 a month.

**Why it stands out for freelancers:** Unlimited 2% on everything means you never have to think about categories. Perfect for freelancers with varied expenses.

**Pros:**
- Unlimited 2% cash back on every purchase
- No spending caps or category restrictions
- $1,200 cash bonus for spending $30,000 in first 3 months
- Free employee cards

**Cons:**
- $150 annual fee
- Charge card (must pay in full each month)
- High spending requirement for signup bonus

**Key specs:** 2% unlimited cash back | $150 annual fee | Pay-in-full charge card

[Get the Capital One Spark Cash Plus](/go/capital-one-spark-cash-plus)

## 3. Blue Business Cash Card from American Express -- Best No Annual Fee

The Blue Business Cash Card earns 2% on the first $50,000 in purchases per year (then 1%), with no annual fee. It also offers 0% intro APR for 12 months, giving freelancers breathing room during a slow season or when making a large business purchase.

**Why it stands out for freelancers:** The combination of 2% cash back and 0% intro APR makes it ideal for freelancers who need flexibility in cash flow.

**Pros:**
- $0 annual fee
- 2% back on first $50,000/year in purchases
- 0% intro APR for 12 months on purchases
- Expanded buying power beyond your credit limit

**Cons:**
- 2% rate drops to 1% after $50,000/year
- No signup bonus currently offered
- Amex not accepted everywhere

**Key specs:** 2%/1% cash back | $0 annual fee | 0% intro APR 12 months

[Get the Blue Business Cash from Amex](/go/amex-blue-business-cash)

## 4. Brex -- Best for Funded Startups and Agencies

Brex is designed for startups and growing businesses. It earns 7x on rideshare, 4x on travel, 3x on restaurants, and 1x on everything else. No personal guarantee required, and it connects directly to accounting software like QuickBooks and Xero.

**Why it stands out for freelancers:** If you run a freelance agency or consultancy with employees and travel, the high multipliers and accounting integrations save real time.

**Pros:**
- No personal guarantee or credit check
- 7x on rideshare, 4x on travel booked via Brex
- Direct integrations with QuickBooks, Xero, NetSuite
- Expense management tools built in

**Cons:**
- Requires a business bank account with meaningful balance
- Not ideal for sole proprietors with low revenue
- Reward redemption options more limited than traditional cards

**Key specs:** 7x/4x/3x/1x rewards | $0 annual fee | No personal guarantee

[Get Brex](/go/brex)

## 5. PayPal Cashback Mastercard -- Best for PayPal Sellers

If you receive payments through PayPal (common for freelance designers, developers, and writers), this card earns 3% cash back on PayPal purchases and 2% everywhere else. The cash back is deposited directly to your PayPal balance.

**Why it stands out for freelancers:** Freelancers who already use PayPal for invoicing get extra rewards on their PayPal spending, creating a closed-loop reward system.

**Pros:**
- 3% on PayPal purchases, 2% everywhere else
- No annual fee
- Cash back deposited to PayPal balance automatically
- No category tracking needed

**Cons:**
- Must have a PayPal account
- 3% rate only applies to purchases made through PayPal
- No signup bonus
- Limited to Mastercard acceptance network

**Key specs:** 3%/2% cash back | $0 annual fee | PayPal balance rewards

[Get the PayPal Cashback Mastercard](/go/paypal-cashback-mastercard)

## How We Chose These Cards

We evaluated credit cards based on criteria that matter most to freelancers:

- **Reward rates on freelancer expenses:** Internet, software, office supplies, and co-working spaces
- **Annual fee value:** Whether rewards earned offset any annual cost
- **Cash flow features:** Intro APR offers and payment flexibility
- **Expense management:** Accounting integrations and employee card controls
- **Accessibility:** Whether sole proprietors can qualify
