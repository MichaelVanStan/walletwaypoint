Buying your first home is one of the biggest financial decisions you will ever make, and the lender you choose matters more than you might think. The difference between two lenders can mean thousands of dollars in fees and tens of thousands in total interest over the life of your loan.

We evaluated major mortgage lenders on rates, fees, first-time buyer programs, and ease of the application process to find five that consistently serve first-time buyers well.

---

## 1. Rocket Mortgage -- Best Online Experience

Rocket Mortgage (formerly Quicken Loans) offers a fully digital mortgage application that you can complete from your phone. Their Verified Approval process gives you a confirmed loan amount, which is stronger than a standard pre-approval when making an offer.

**Why it stands out for first-time buyers:** The entirely online process removes the intimidation factor of in-person meetings, and Rocket's interface guides you through each step with plain-language explanations.

**Pros:**
- Fully online application with real-time status tracking
- Verified Approval (fully underwritten before you shop)
- Consistently rated top for customer satisfaction
- Conventional, FHA, VA, and jumbo loans available

**Cons:**
- Rates can be slightly higher than local lenders
- No USDA loans
- Origination fees apply
- Limited in-person support

**Key specs:** Conventional, FHA, VA, Jumbo | 620+ credit score for most loans | Fully digital process

[Check Rates at Rocket Mortgage](/go/rocket-mortgage)

## 2. Better Mortgage -- Best for Low Fees

Better Mortgage charges no lender fees -- no origination fees, no application fees, no underwriting fees. This can save first-time buyers $1,000 to $3,000 at closing compared to traditional lenders.

**Why it stands out for first-time buyers:** When you are stretching to afford a down payment, every dollar in closing costs matters. Better's no-fee model means more of your savings go toward the home.

**Pros:**
- No origination fees, no lender fees
- Fully digital pre-approval in as little as 3 minutes
- Price match guarantee on rates
- Conventional, FHA, and jumbo loans available

**Cons:**
- No physical branches for in-person meetings
- No VA or USDA loans
- Customer service can be inconsistent during high volume
- Limited refinance options compared to larger lenders

**Key specs:** Conventional, FHA, Jumbo | No lender fees | 620+ credit score | Digital process

[Check Rates at Better Mortgage](/go/better-mortgage)

## 3. Chase Home Lending -- Best for Existing Customers

Chase offers competitive rates across conventional, FHA, and VA loans, with additional rate discounts and closing cost grants for existing Chase banking customers. Their DreaMaker program provides up to $5,000 in closing cost assistance for low-to-moderate income buyers.

**Why it stands out for first-time buyers:** If you already bank with Chase, the relationship discounts and $5,000 DreaMaker grant can meaningfully reduce your upfront costs.

**Pros:**
- Rate discounts for existing Chase customers
- DreaMaker program: up to $5,000 in closing cost assistance
- Physical branches for in-person support
- Conventional, FHA, and VA loans available

**Cons:**
- Must be a Chase banking customer for best rates
- No USDA loans
- Online experience not as polished as Rocket or Better
- DreaMaker has income and geographic limits

**Key specs:** Conventional, FHA, VA | DreaMaker $5K grant | 620+ credit score | Branch + digital

[Check Rates at Chase](/go/chase-mortgage)

## 4. Navy Federal Credit Union -- Best for Military and Veterans

Navy Federal offers some of the lowest mortgage rates available to military members, veterans, and their families. Their HomeBuyers Choice program requires no down payment, no PMI, and no origination fee -- a combination that is nearly impossible to find elsewhere.

**Why it stands out for first-time buyers:** For eligible military families, the HomeBuyers Choice program eliminates the three biggest barriers to homeownership: the down payment, PMI, and lender fees.

**Pros:**
- HomeBuyers Choice: no down payment, no PMI, no origination fee
- Consistently among the lowest rates for VA loans
- In-person and digital support
- Conventional, VA, and jumbo loans available

**Cons:**
- Membership restricted to military, veterans, and families
- No FHA or USDA loans
- Branch network smaller than national banks
- Application process can be slower than digital-first lenders

**Key specs:** Conventional, VA, Jumbo | No PMI options | Military membership required | $0 down available

[Check Rates at Navy Federal](/go/navy-federal-mortgage)

## 5. Guild Mortgage -- Best for Low Credit Scores

Guild Mortgage specializes in government-backed loans and works with borrowers who have credit scores as low as 540 for FHA loans. They also offer down payment assistance programs in many states, making homeownership accessible to buyers who might be turned away elsewhere.

**Why it stands out for first-time buyers:** If your credit score is below 620, most lenders will not work with you. Guild's willingness to underwrite FHA loans at 540+ and their down payment assistance partnerships fill a critical gap.

**Pros:**
- FHA loans with credit scores as low as 540
- Down payment assistance programs in many states
- Conventional, FHA, VA, and USDA loans available
- In-person loan officers in 49 states

**Cons:**
- Rates may be higher for low-credit-score borrowers
- Origination fees apply
- Online experience not as strong as digital-first lenders
- Not the most competitive on jumbo loans

**Key specs:** Conventional, FHA, VA, USDA | 540+ credit score for FHA | Down payment assistance | Branch network

[Check Rates at Guild Mortgage](/go/guild-mortgage)

## How We Chose These Lenders

We evaluated mortgage lenders based on criteria most important to first-time buyers:

- **Rates and fees:** Total cost of borrowing, including origination fees and closing costs
- **First-time buyer programs:** Down payment assistance, closing cost grants, and low-down-payment options
- **Credit score requirements:** Minimum scores for approval and rate tiers
- **Ease of process:** Application experience, communication, and closing timeline
- **Loan variety:** Conventional, FHA, VA, USDA, and jumbo availability
