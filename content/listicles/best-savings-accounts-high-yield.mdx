A high-yield savings account pays 10 to 15 times more interest than a traditional bank savings account. In 2026, the best high-yield savings accounts offer APYs above 4.50%, FDIC insurance up to $250,000, and no monthly fees or minimum balance requirements.

We compared rates, fees, minimum deposits, and features from dozens of banks and picked five accounts that stand out for everyday savers.

---

## 1. Marcus by Goldman Sachs -- Best Overall High-Yield Savings

Marcus consistently offers one of the highest APYs in the market with no minimum deposit, no monthly fees, and no maximum balance limits. The account is straightforward: you deposit money, it earns interest, and there are no gimmicks.

**Why it stands out:** Competitive APY with zero fees and zero minimums. Marcus has maintained consistently top-tier rates for years.

**Pros:**
- No minimum deposit to open
- No monthly maintenance fees
- No maximum balance cap on APY
- Easy transfers to and from external banks

**Cons:**
- No physical branches
- No checking account or debit card available
- Mobile app is functional but basic
- No joint accounts available online

**Key specs:** 4.50% APY | $0 minimum deposit | FDIC insured | No monthly fees

[Open a Marcus Account](/go/marcus-savings)

---

## 2. Ally Bank Online Savings -- Best for Everyday Banking

Ally Bank offers a strong APY alongside a full suite of banking products. If you want a high-yield savings account that lives alongside checking, CDs, and investing -- all in one app -- Ally is the most complete option.

**Why it stands out:** Ally is more than a savings account. The "buckets" feature lets you organize savings goals within one account, and the round-up feature automatically saves spare change from Ally checking purchases.

**Pros:**
- No minimum deposit, no monthly fees
- "Buckets" feature for goal-based saving within one account
- Round-up savings from Ally checking
- Full banking ecosystem (checking, CDs, investing)

**Cons:**
- APY slightly lower than rate-leader competitors
- No physical branches
- Wire transfers not supported for savings accounts
- Customer service wait times can be long

**Key specs:** 4.35% APY | $0 minimum deposit | FDIC insured | Buckets feature

[Open an Ally Savings Account](/go/ally-savings)

---

## 3. Discover Online Savings -- Best for Cash Back Pairing

Discover's Online Savings account earns a competitive APY and pairs naturally with the Discover cashback debit card and Discover credit cards. If you are already in the Discover ecosystem, this is a seamless addition.

**Why it stands out:** Discover offers 24/7 US-based customer service (no overseas call centers), and the account integrates smoothly with other Discover products.

**Pros:**
- No minimum deposit, no monthly fees
- 24/7 US-based customer service
- Pairs well with Discover checking and credit cards
- FDIC insured up to $250,000

**Cons:**
- APY competitive but not always the highest
- No physical branches
- Limited third-party app integrations
- Mobile check deposit limits are low for new accounts

**Key specs:** 4.30% APY | $0 minimum deposit | FDIC insured | US-based support

[Open a Discover Savings Account](/go/discover-savings)

---

## 4. CIT Bank Platinum Savings -- Best for Larger Balances

CIT Bank's Platinum Savings account offers a top-tier APY for balances of $5,000 or more. If you have a healthy emergency fund or are saving for a major goal, the higher APY on larger balances earns you more than flat-rate competitors.

**Why it stands out:** The tiered rate structure rewards savers who keep $5,000+ in the account. For balances above that threshold, CIT consistently offers among the highest rates available.

**Pros:**
- Top-tier APY for balances of $5,000+
- No monthly maintenance fees
- FDIC insured through First Citizens BancShares
- Multiple CD and savings products available

**Cons:**
- Lower APY for balances under $5,000
- $100 minimum deposit to open
- Limited banking products (no checking)
- Website interface feels dated

**Key specs:** 4.55% APY (on $5K+) | $100 minimum deposit | FDIC insured | Tiered rates

[Open a CIT Platinum Savings Account](/go/cit-bank-savings)

## 5. Wealthfront Cash Account -- Best for Investing Integration

Wealthfront's Cash Account is technically a brokerage cash sweep, but it functions like a high-yield savings account with FDIC insurance up to $8 million (through partner banks). If you also invest with Wealthfront, your cash and investments live in one clean interface.

**Why it stands out:** FDIC coverage up to $8 million is unmatched. If you want both saving and automated investing in one platform, Wealthfront eliminates the need for multiple apps.

**Pros:**
- FDIC insured up to $8 million through partner banks
- No minimum balance or monthly fees
- Integrates with Wealthfront automated investing
- Unlimited transfers

**Cons:**
- Not a traditional bank account (brokerage cash sweep)
- No checking account or debit card
- Interest rate may fluctuate more than traditional banks
- No physical branches

**Key specs:** 4.50% APY | $0 minimum | FDIC insured up to $8M | Investing integration

[Open a Wealthfront Cash Account](/go/wealthfront-cash)

## How We Chose These Accounts

We evaluated high-yield savings accounts based on:

- **APY:** The interest rate you actually earn, compared to the national average
- **Fees:** Monthly maintenance fees, minimum balance fees, and transfer fees
- **Minimum deposit:** How much you need to open and maintain the account
- **FDIC insurance:** Verified coverage up to at least $250,000
- **Features:** Goal tracking, mobile app quality, and banking integrations

Rates shown are current as of March 2026 and may change. See our [editorial standards](/editorial-standards) for how we evaluate financial products.
